Background and Motivation

Human-Computer Interaction aims to fundamentally improve the way humans interact with technology. Heads-Up Computing is the ambitious vision of the NUS-HCI Lab to introduce a new paradigm of interaction with technology. Its key concept involves a suite of wearable technology that offers a style of interaction compatible with natural and intuitive human movements. Once realised, Heads-Up Computing will transform the way we work and live. Messaging apps are an ideal direction to further the Heads-Up Computing vision due to their importance, popularity and ubiquity. Messaging apps are the most widely used smartphone app, with over 3.3 billion monthly users on WhatsApp and Facebook Messenger combined [6]. In the current day, using a messaging app requires that we look down at a mobile device. This style of interaction imposes constraints on how we can engage with our other activities as it undermines our ability to be situationally aware and requires both hands and our eyes.
